Headmaster Amma

Amma bringing her 30 years of classroom precision and passion into the kitchen.

From English Grammar to Spicy Grinding

"If a job is worth doing, it is worth doing perfectly." This was the motto that defined Amma's 30-year career as an English headmaster.

In her classrooms, spelling errors were red-lined, pronunciation was polished, and rules were respected. Amma believed that details matter, and that a disciplined foundation builds character.

When she retired and moved to Austin, she brought that exact same standard into her home kitchen. She noticed that many store-bought pickles and podis tasted generic or contained artificial preservatives to extend shelf life. To Amma, that was a "failing grade." She set out to recreate the authentic, bold, and complex flavor profiles of her home in Andhra Pradesh, using traditional techniques.

Personal Sourcing & Care

Hands-On Passion from Source to Jar

Amma doesn't rely on bulk wholesale suppliers. She personally hand-picks every ingredient from farm to market.

From visiting organic fields to harvest fresh greens and vegetables, to exploring local markets to touch, smell, and select the finest whole spices and raw sour mangoes, Amma oversees every step of the sourcing.

Her hands-on passion extends beyond the kitchen walls. She hand-inspects the packaging, manages batch quality, and personally supervises the distribution of every jar to ensure that what reaches your table is nothing short of authentic Andhra perfection.

The Kitchen Code

Amma's rules for an authentic jar

01

Inspection of Raw Materials

Just like verifying homework, Amma personally inspects every batch of raw mangoes, fresh sorrel leaves, and whole red chillies. If a mango has a soft spot or a curry leaf isn't crisp, it is rejected immediately.

02

Traditional Timelines

No cutting corners. The sun-drying of red chillies and fenugreek must take place over specific hours to extract maximum aroma. Grinding is done in stone grinders, refusing electric high-heat blenders that scorch spices.

03

Balanced Spice Science

Andhra food is famous for its heat, but Amma insists on balance. Each recipe uses cold-pressed sesame oil to temper the Guntur chillies, creating a satisfying, layered burn rather than single-note spiciness.
